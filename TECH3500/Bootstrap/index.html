Hynes Hall

The new Hynes Hall includes a diverse array of state-of-the-art resources for business, public administration and criminal justice students.

Miron Student Center

In addition to office spaces and meeting rooms for many groups, the Miron Student Center offers a game room, food court, Cougars Den, Smashburger, Auntie Anne's Pretzels, Outtakes convenience store and the Little Theatre.

CAS

Kean University President Dawood Farahi developed the concept for the Center for Academic Success in 2003 to ensure excellence and accessibility for the entire Kean University student population. The Center for Academic Success began as a consolidation of the Career Services Center, the First Year Center and the Learning Assistance Program, and expanded to improve access to student academic support services.

Hennings Hall

The Dorothy and George Hennings College of Science, Mathematics and Technology (HCSMT) contains all of Kean's programs in the technological and life sciences, including Biology, Chemistry, Computer Science, Information Technology, Computational Science, Mathematics, Physics, Molecular Biology, Biotechnology and Environmental Science. Many programs provide trajectories into clinical health science graduate programs such as Medical, Dental, Occupational Therapy, Physical Therapy, and Physician Assistant Studies.

Registration for Fall 2023 full-term courses, first-half courses and first-half Kean Online courses has concluded. Registration for Fall 2023 second-half courses and second-half Kean Online courses is open, but students may only register by visiting the One Stop Service Center or completing the Fall 2023 Second-Half Term Registration Request Form in accordance with the deadlines below: Second-Half Term Courses Late Registration Deadline: Wednesday, November 8 (Classes begin Tuesday, October 31, 2023) Second-Half Online Courses Late Registration Deadline: Friday, November 4 (Classes begin Tuesday, October 31, 2023)
Students registering during the Late Registration period (after the published Payment Due Date) will be assessed a late registration fee of $105.00. Newly admitted students who have been accepted by Kean University two weeks prior to the payment due date and thereafter will be exempt from this policy. A Late Registration Fee will NOT be charged to students making course changes, including adding and dropping courses, after the published Payment Due Date.
